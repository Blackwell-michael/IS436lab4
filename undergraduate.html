Undergraduate Programs in Information Systems

UMBC offers a Bachelor of Science in Information Systems that combines computing, business processes, and human centered design. Students learn programming, systems analysis, databases, networking, analytics, and project management while developing communication and teamwork skills valued by employers.

Program Highlights

  • Strong foundation in technical and analytical skills.
  • Emphasis on real projects with regional organizations.
  • Opportunities for internships, research, and honors.
  • Supportive advising and tutoring resources.

Admission and Advising

Students may enter as Information Systems majors or transition after meeting prerequisite requirements. Academic advisors assist with course planning, transfer credit review, and graduation preparation.

Sample Curriculum Snapshot

A typical plan includes foundational math and computing, core Information Systems courses, and electives in areas such as cybersecurity, health IT, data science, and user experience design.
